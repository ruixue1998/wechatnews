Global 6G Takes Another Step Forward: Standardization Work Commences

According to People’s Post and Telecommunications News, from August 25 to 29, the first 3GPP 6G Radio Access Network (RAN) working group meeting was held in Bangalore, India.

It is reported that this meeting marks the official launch of 3GPP’s standardization research on the 6G radio access network. 19 related proposals submitted by 17 global mainstream technology companies have moved 6G from the “conceptual exploration” stage to the substantive stage of “technological breakthroughs”.

This meeting focused on two agendas closely related to near-field technology, based on the core needs for 6G technology implementation:

  • The first is a 6G special agenda, focusing on the basic design of the 6G radio access (6GR) air interface: clarifying the overall technical framework of the 6GR air interface and determining the core dimensions of air interface evaluation assumptions;
  • The second is the revision of the 3GPP channel model, optimizing the parameters of the near-field channel model in the 7-24GHz frequency band.

The report mentions that judging from the proposals of 17 companies, the global industry has formed a technological exploration direction of “focusing on pain points and having different focuses”:

  • Huawei and HiSilicon’s joint proposal directly targets frequency band protocols, explicitly mentioning the need for adaptation of the near-field channel model in the 7-24GHz frequency band, providing protocol-level reference for high-frequency near-field communication;
  • ZTE and SK Telecom focus on the evaluation system, suggesting that the near-field channel model in TR 38.901 be included in the 6GR evaluation, and that near-field communication be listed as one of the core solutions for multi-antenna technology in the early stages (Day-1) of 6G;
  • Xiaomi proposes the idea of “fine-grained beam management” in its proposal, suggesting the design of dedicated codebooks for near-field users, and promoting the inclusion of near-field characteristics in FR3 (third frequency band) channel modeling; vivo emphasizes the impact of “spatial nonlinear effects,” considering it key to improving 6G spectrum efficiency; OPPO further clarifies that FR3 should become a new evaluation scenario for 6GR, and the synergistic effect of massive MIMO and near-field technology needs to be tested;
  • Nvidia focuses on enhancing massive MIMO (mMIMO), suggesting research on the switching mechanism between near-field and far-field users, and optimizing reference signal design to adapt to the interdependence of “distance-angle”;
  • Apple proposes a differentiated modeling approach for the 7GHz frequency band—near-field MIMO design needs to consider spherical wave propagation, while far-field can flexibly choose;
  • Qualcomm emphasizes the principle of “scenario adaptation,” believing that the far-field model is still the baseline, and the near-field model needs to be enabled in combination with actual deployment needs.

iPhone 17 Air in Mainland China Reportedly to Support eSIM, Weibo CEO Retweets

Recently, blogger “Chills Ya Ya” posted that they learned from relevant sources at China Unicom that the company headquarters has issued a notice to provide business support for Apple’s eSIM project.

According to the information revealed, the upcoming iPhone 17 Air is expected to support eSIM services in Mainland China, but it is uncertain whether it will be available at launch.

Last night, Sina Weibo CEO Wang Gaofei (“Lai Qu Zhi Jian”) retweeted the relevant content with the hashtag “Daily Advertisement”.
